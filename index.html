July 2024

NWO Career grant | Veni

I am very happy to announce that my Veni project ASCEND (Advancing Subsurface Characterization via Ensemble Nonlinear Data assimilation) has been granted! In this project, I will research adaptation and localization strategies that can render ensemble transport filters significantly more efficient. This will yield more reliable subsurface characterizations, which will improve the safety and efficiency of many important operations for the energy transition, from geothermal engineering to hydrogen storage. PhD vacancy on Nonlinear Data Assimilation

I am hiring! Are you passionate about advancing the energy transition? Are you curious about methods that will help us to learn more about the subsurface? Together with my colleagues Berent Lunde and Femke Vossepoel, I am looking for a motivated PhD student to join our project on adaptive nonlinear data assimilation in subsurface systems. In this collaboration between Delft University of Technology and Equinor, we will develop an exciting new framework to improve our understanding of complex subsurface environments, crucial for safe drinking water, geothermal energy, and hydrogen storage.
